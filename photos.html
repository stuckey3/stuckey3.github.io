A Summer In The Rockie Mountains

For the duration of the summer 0f 2021 I worked as a camp counselor for Camp Granite Lake. A blink you might miss it safehaven in the foothills of Boulder CO. where kids could engulf themselves in a authentic and welcoming setting. Kids could explore and develop skills in outdoor activites while creating frienships and connections with others. As a Camp Counselor I honed practical skills as a leader, well practiced communicator, and got to feed my adventourous spirit in a environment that allowed me to follow my love for the outdoors. When I wasn't teaching kids how to rock climb or kayak through local rivers, I was able to venture off in to the mountains of Colorado and first-handly experience the plethora of natural beauty the great state offers.

Moments before being surronded by a fast moving snowstorm while hiking through a mountain pass in Georgetown CO. Pictured is a local snowboarder who was planning his summit down the mountain.
As a certified rock climbing belayer, I was in charge of garnering kids trust and coaching them while they climbed up our local rock face.
Caught in the act of dragging a canoe in to the icy cold waters of our local alpine lake. Charging up the campers to delve in to the unfriendly waters was always a challenge I welcomed.
After a grueling and unapolegtic hike up Mt. Bierstadt, one of CO. many 14,000 thousand foot mountains, ample time was spent taking in the phenomenal views of the surrodning peaks and valleys.
While heights do tend to scare me a tiny bit, I did not let my phobia get in the way of enjoying this beautiful sunset over the continental divide.

Roadtrip Out West

In the summer of 2020, me and four of my friends went on a two week roadtrip. This tripped spanned states such as South Dakota, Wyoming, Colorado, and Utah. We went to several landmark sites such as the Grand Tetons, Zion National Park, Rocky Mountain National Park, and the Black Hills. It was a trip of strenous hikes with beautiful views, subpar campfire food, and great memories. Here are some pictures of me my pals enjoying the picterque nature that our country has to offer.

The views of Zion were quite special but the gutwrenching heat that never dipped under 90 lead to many stops for water and self-doubting conversations
Our cabin in Silverton CO gave us Gen Z kids a run for our money. Without any electricity, running water, service, or WIFI our time was spent playing cards, reading books, and figuring out how purify river water.
In Silverton CO. the rolling mountains were never-ending. Many a days were spent walking along mountaintops and comparing the natural beauty that the west has to offer compared to never-ending farm fields of the midwest
While visitng the Grand Tetons we reserved a day to scale the mountains on a grueling 10 mile hike. Luckily a lake awaited at the top where we could take a dip in the freezing water.
When driving through the barren landscape of Wyoming, finding any sign of local civilazation can be a challenge. Stops on the side of the highway to go to the bathroom or brush ones teeth was a reality we had to get used to.
A well-welcomed stop in the shade after miles of walking through back-breaking heat- Existential thoughts ensued
